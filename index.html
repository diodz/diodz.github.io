Diego A. Diaz

Applied economist and data scientist

I work on empirical questions in markets, public policy, and economic measurement. My work combines econometrics and large-scale data pipelines, with an emphasis on reproducible analysis and careful interpretation.

I am especially interested in settings where public data, administrative records, and marketplace data can be used to understand economic behavior and inform decisions. I currently work at Charles River Associates.

Published Research and Working Papers

The Internet Business Pulse: Public Data, Digital Commerce, and the Limits of Monthly Nowcasting

Constructs a monthly public-data indicator of internet-facing business activity from Census retail, e-commerce, and Business Formation Statistics, then validates it against quarterly retail e-commerce growth.

Economic indicators, nowcasting, PCA, public Census data, reproducibility. PDF

The Small-Business Sensitivity Index: Local Retail Demand and High-Propensity Business Formation

Links Census Business Formation Statistics to Monthly State Retail Sales to measure how high-propensity business applications comove with local retail demand across U.S. states.

State panels, fixed effects, clustered inference, public data. PDF

From Offline to Online? Local Retail Shocks and the Persistence of Digital Business Formation

Tests whether early COVID offline retail disruption predicted subsequent employer-oriented business applications, distinguishing local exposure from broader sectoral reallocation.

Local projections, difference-in-differences diagnostics, digital adoption, business formation. PDF

Institutional Quality and Pandemic Resilience: Cross-Country Evidence on Health and Economic Outcomes during COVID-19

Online preprint with Cristian Larroulet and Andres Marticorena examining how pre-pandemic institutional quality is associated with COVID-19 mortality, vaccination rollout, and economic recovery across countries.

Governance, state capacity, cross-country evidence, pandemic resilience. Preprint

Administrative capacity and public transfer targeting

Empirical and structural research pipeline linking rollout exposure, beneficiary information, local capacity, and cross-state validation in public transfer systems.

Panel data, public administrative data, structural modeling, reproducibility. PDF

Short-term rental regulation and housing markets

Replication package studying platform scale, housing scarcity, and short-term rental regulation using Airbnb listings, Zillow indices, New York City registration records, ACS housing data, and policy-event coding.

Event studies, synthetic control, spatial joins, public-data manifests. PDF

Local AI innovation and firm dynamics

Commuting-zone panel on AI patenting and firm dynamics using PatentsView, Census geography, and Business Dynamics Statistics. The current framing is descriptive, with event-study and Bartik diagnostics reported as supplementary checks.

Fixed effects, public data, validation reports. PDF

An agent-based model of school choice with information asymmetries

Published in the Journal of Simulation. The paper studies school-choice policies with heterogeneous agents and information asymmetries, using data from Santiago schools to simulate the 2010 traffic-light information policy.

Simulation, optimization, policy communication. Article

Another case of the middle-income trap: Chile, 1900-1939

Published in Revista de Historia Economica / Journal of Iberian and Latin American Economic History. The paper combines economic history with quantitative evidence on Chile's early twentieth-century growth slowdown.

Economic history, synthetic control, institutional change. Article
